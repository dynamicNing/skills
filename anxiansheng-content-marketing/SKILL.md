# 安先生内容营销课

## When To Use

Use this skill when the user wants to apply or explain 安先生的三天内容营销/自媒体课程, especially:

- 账号定位、内容方向、内容支点
- 5A 人群经营：A1 了解、A2 吸引、A3 问询、A4 行动、A5 拥护
- 四类内容：曝光、引流获客、成交、人设
- 卖点开发、需求创造、场景化表达
- 短视频选题、周排期、单条内容设计、发布复盘
- AI 辅助内容生产但不替代判断

For full course notes, read `references/course-summary.md` only when the user needs detail, examples, a comprehensive summary, or you need to ground an implementation in the original framework.

## Core Thesis

Content does not directly make money. Content first changes user mindsets; transactions happen later through products, services, private traffic, live rooms, stores, consultations, or repeat purchases.

The practical question is never just "how do I get traffic?" It is:

- Who am I speaking to?
- What mindset or relationship stage are they in?
- What should this one piece of content accomplish?
- Why should they trust me instead of someone else?
- What action should they take after watching?

## Framework

### Day1: Build The Content Fulcrum

Use the six-part content fulcrum before planning content:

1. **生命体验**: real experiences that make the creator's views credible.
2. **影像资源**: face, temperament, location, workplace, product, people, scenes, and borrowable resources.
3. **课题问题**: the specific question the account keeps answering. Prefer "small problem, large demand".
4. **价值主张**: what the creator believes, opposes, and wants to change for the user.
5. **理想用户**: one concrete person, not a demographic label.
6. **关系构建**: the speaking relationship and scene, such as clinic consultation, workplace aside, coffee-table chat, or friend-to-friend advice.

Then choose one or two competition strategies:

- **正**: win a smaller battlefield where the creator has clear advantage.
- **反**: oppose a mainstream view or route.
- **和**: combine two values, such as practical knowledge plus emotional resonance.
- **跨**: use another discipline to answer the same question.
- **借**: borrow momentum from topics, people, places, books, events, or scenes.
- **弱**: use honest limitations or shared problems to create connection, without selling misery.

### Day2/Day3: Assign One Job Per Content Piece

Classify content by purpose, not by accidental result:

| Type | Job | 5A Stage | Main Check |
|---|---|---|---|
| 曝光内容 | Let more relevant people see the account | A1 | Does the audience include precise users? |
| 引流获客内容 | Make users want to ask, comment, DM, enter live room, or inquire | A3 | Does it create desire before pushing decisions? |
| 成交内容 | Make ready users buy now | A4 | Is the buying reason, risk removal, and action clear? |
| 人设内容 | Build trust and a lasting impression | A1-A5 | Does it provide value and concrete trust evidence? |

Do not ask one piece of content to do everything. If it is exposure, do not force a hard sale. If it is acquisition, do not over-explain price and qualifications before desire exists. If it is persona, it must still provide value. If it is closing, be direct about the action.

## Workflows

### Account Diagnosis

When diagnosing an account or content plan:

1. Identify what the business sells, who pays, and where transactions happen.
2. Determine the current bottleneck:
   - Nobody sees it: needs 曝光.
   - People watch but nobody asks: needs 引流获客.
   - People ask but do not buy: needs 成交 or trust.
   - People follow but do not believe: needs 人设.
3. Define one concrete seed user.
4. Check the six content fulcrum elements for mismatch.
5. Recommend the next 3-5 minimum viable content tests.

### Sell Point Development

Translate industry assumptions into user-perceived value:

1. List production, selection, delivery, service, and after-sales details.
2. Ask like an outsider: why does this step exist?
3. Keep details that affect a result the user cares about.
4. Rewrite each sell point in three layers:
   - 功能层: what problem it solves.
   - 体验层: what usage feels like.
   - 象征层: what identity, taste, or belonging it expresses.
5. Put every sell point into a concrete scene: who, when, where, doing what, with what surrounding elements.

### Single Content Design

Before drafting a post or short video, fill this:

```markdown
内容类型：曝光 / 引流获客 / 成交 / 人设
第一目的：
目标用户：
用户当前状态：
核心一句话：
具体场景：
目标动作：
成功判断标准：
```

Then structure it:

1. Open with the topic, desire, conflict, scene, or question.
2. Explain one core logic only.
3. Add concrete details, scenes, cases, or trust evidence.
4. End with one action only.

### Content Planning

Choose proportions by bottleneck, not fixed formulas. As a starting point:

- 普通个人账号: 2 曝光 + 2 获客 + 2 人设 + 1 成交 per week.
- 高客单 / 非标品 / To B: 1 曝光 + 2 获客 + 3 人设 + 1 成交 per week.
- 本地生活 / 低客单 / 即时消费: 1 曝光 + 3 获客 + 1 人设 + 2 成交 per week.

Adjust weekly:

- Low reach: add exposure.
- Reach but no inquiries: add acquisition and desire scenes.
- Inquiries but no purchases: add trust and closing content.
- Weak relationship: add persona content with concrete evidence.

### Review

Review by original purpose before metrics:

- 曝光: Did it enter a public topic or adjacent circle? Did precise users appear?
- 获客: Did users ask? Were the questions business-relevant?
- 成交: Did ready users buy or ask final decision questions?
- 人设: Did comments become higher quality? Did users understand why this creator is credible?

Only after that inspect plays, completion, likes, comments, saves, DMs, and conversion.

## Output Patterns

For summaries, preserve the system logic. Do not over-compress into slogans.

For content calendars, use a table with:

```markdown
| 日期 | 内容类型 | 选题 | 目标用户 | 切入角度 | 场景/卖点 | 目标动作 |
```

For topic ideation, include:

```markdown
| 内容类型 | 选题 | 用户此刻关心什么 | 为什么会看 | 看完要做什么 |
```

For single scripts, state the content type and purpose before the script.

## Guardrails

- Do not reduce the course to traffic hacks, hook formulas, or platform tricks.
- Do not use generic personas like "25-40 岁女性" unless the user explicitly wants broad segmentation; convert them into concrete seed users.
- Do not treat "专业、靠谱、服务好、便宜" as sell points without evidence and scenes.
- Do not overuse pain-point anxiety. Prefer showing the better state after the problem is solved.
- Do not blindly copy competitors; point out hidden variables such as paid traffic, private traffic, team, margin, and backend conversion.
- Do not let AI replace the creator's experience. Use AI for decomposition, options, comparison, and expansion; require human judgment and private experience in the final content.
